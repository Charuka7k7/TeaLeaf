Overview

A Tea Harvested Under Moonlight

Silver Tips is arguably the most extraordinary tea produced anywhere in the world. Made exclusively from the silvery, unopened buds of the tea plant — never the leaves — it is harvested by hand on only two nights each year: the full moon nights of March and August, when the lunar cycle is believed to intensify the buds' essential oils and natural sweetness.

The pickers work in the pre-dawn darkness using only the light of the full moon to guide them. The delicate buds, covered in fine white hairs that give them their silver appearance, are collected in small bamboo baskets and brought to the factory within hours of plucking for gentle sun-drying. No heat, no rolling, no intervention — just nature doing what it does best.

The result is a tea of breathtaking rarity and refinement, produced in quantities so small that it commands among the highest prices of any Ceylon tea on the international market. To drink Silver Tips is to hold history, tradition, and nature's finest artistry in a single cup.

The Full Moon Tradition

The practice of harvesting Silver Tips only under the full moon dates back generations. Planters believe the moon draws more essential oils to the surface of the bud, creating a more fragrant, more complex tea. Scientific studies have begun to support this — plants show measurably higher moisture and volatile compound activity during full moons.

Sensory Profile

Flavor, Aroma and Character

star

Taste

Extraordinarily delicate and naturally sweet, like liquid honey with notes of ripe melon, white flower petals, and a whisper of vanilla. There is zero bitterness or astringency. The sweetness intensifies as the cup cools, revealing new dimensions with each sip.

air

Aroma

The dry buds carry an intoxicating fragrance, sweet, floral, almost powdery. When steeped, the aroma deepens into notes of wild honey, jasmine blossom, and fresh highland dew. Many describe it as the most beautiful fragrance in the tea world.

colorize

Liquor Color

Pale silver-gold to the faintest blush of champagne — completely transparent and brilliant. The extraordinary clarity of Silver Tips liquor is a testament to the purity of using only unopened buds. It is, quite simply, one of the most beautiful liquors in the tea world.

Brewing Guide

How to Brew Silver Tips

1

Use the Coolest Water

65–70°C only. Silver Tips is the most delicate tea in the world and will turn bitter or watery if exposed to hot water. A temperature-controlled kettle is ideal; otherwise let boiled water cool for 8–10 minutes.

2

Be Generous with the Buds

Use a tablespoon (4–5g) of Silver Tips per 200ml, the buds are light and airy. Use a glass teapot or infuser if possible so you can watch the beautiful buds unfurl in the water.

3

Steep for 1–2 Minutes Only

Steeping time is critical. Remove the buds at exactly 1 minute for a whisper light cup; 2 minutes for a little more body and sweetness. The buds can be re-steeped 2–3 times.

4

Sip Slowly — Undisturbed

Never add milk, sugar, or lemon. Silver Tips is best appreciated in silence, in a good cup, at room temperature as it cools. Allow the flavor to evolve from floral to honeyed as you sit with it.

Health Benefits

check_circle

Exceptional Antioxidant Density

Being made only from the youngest buds, the most metabolically active part of the plant, Silver Tips contains the highest concentration of antioxidants of any tea, including significantly more EGCG than typical white teas.

check_circle

Anti-Inflammatory

High levels of flavonoids help reduce chronic inflammation, a root cause of many modern diseases including cardiovascular disease, arthritis, and certain cancers.

check_circle

Virtually Caffeine-Free

Silver Tips contains only trace amounts of caffeine, making it perfect for evening enjoyment, for those sensitive to caffeine, and for children and the elderly.

check_circle

Supports Immunity

Rich in catechins and amino acids that bolster immune function, Silver Tips has long been used in traditional Sri Lankan wellness practices as a restorative tonic.
